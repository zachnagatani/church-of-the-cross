About Us

Mission

Church of the Cross is a community that is committed to following Jesus, loving people, and engaging San Diego with the gospel.


Vision

At Church of the Cross our vision is to cultivate passionate Christ-centered disciples through local influence, domestic church planting, and global missions.


DNA (Values)

Christ-Centered

It all begins and ends with Jesus (Rev. 22:13)! We believe that the person and work of Christ is the hermeneutical lens by which we interpret Scripture (Luke 24:44), and engage our culture. It is the message about who Jesus is and what he accomplished through his atoning work of the cross.

While all churches will affirm this at some level, we believe this isn’t merely something to affirm, but rather the central purpose for the universal and local church community. The good news of Jesus guides and directs our vision, our ministry, and our cultural engagement.

Cross-Shaped

The cross of Christ changes the way we know and worship God. It was the means by which the Father sent his Son to die for the sins of the world. Jesus affirms that in order to follow Jesus we are to bear the cross as well (Mark 8:34-38; Luke 14:27).

Similarly, Paul shows us that just as Jesus bore the shame of the cross in his humanity as a slave, likewise those who call Jesus Lord and King are to lives that reflect the cross (Phil. 2:5-11). We call this a living a cruciform life . A cruciform life is all about self-denial, sacrifice, and the commitment to living and dying in the service of others. It is a life shaped like and shaped by the cross of Christ..

Family-Oriented

We are committed to the concept of the church as God’s surrogate family. The metaphors for the church being “God’s children” or the “brothers and sisters of Christ” are used with a purpose (Mark 3:34-35; Acts 2:42-47; Rom. 8:14-17). In an individualistic culture where the family structure has been redefined, and often times has been destroyed, we believe God has established a better family, one which all who call upon the name of Jesus as Lord is a part of and committed to.

Mission-Minded

A commitment to the gospel is a commitment to God’s purposes for the world. Jesus’ purpose for coming into earth was to begin the process of establishing God’s kingdom. Since the culmination of God’s kingdom plan doesn’t occur until Jesus’ second coming, the church is participating with God until that full completion. This is the mission of God, and we as church are enthusiastic about this participation.


Strategy

Christ-Centered Preaching

Since we believe the cross of Christ rests as the basis of our ministry, and historically has been the mission of the Christian church for two thousand years, we believe that the proclamation of God’s word every week is crucial in submitting ourselves and committing ourselves to the liberating gospel of Christ.

Weekly Community Groups

We are committed to the weekly gathering of corporate worship where hear God speak through his word, and partake in his body and blood – historically known as Communion, or the Lord’s Supper. In addition, we also meet weekly in smaller communities known as community groups. These groups are designed to continue in the encouragement and growth of the individual with in the larger church community. In these groups we share a meal, continue in the study of God’s word and its significance in our lives, as well as prayer for particular needs. We do not see these groups as an additional program, but rather one necessary part of our corporate identity as a community. In other words, it’s a lifestyle.

Relational Evangelism

Our desire is not to reproduce the individualism prevalent within our culture, but rather to see holistic faith occur within the church, thus inviting people into a vibrant community. We plan to accomplish this by participating in the following:

  1. HEAD (Intellectual) – Participate with other churches, and community events, that give a platform to make the intellectual ideas of the Christian faith known to the broader culture.

  2. HEART (Relational) – Evangelism and discipleship go hand in hand. Intellectual discussions (HEAD) should hopefully give room for relational (HEART) interaction.

  3. HANDS (Practical) – Sometimes the greatest thing we can do in order to make the saving grace of Jesus known is to serve the practical needs of the community. While we desire most of the practical needs to be met through the community groups, we will also interact the local needs of our community accordingly.

In addition, the process of making disciples should result in further gospel ministry. We desire to see an impact of the gospel in local businesses, domestic church planting, and global missions, as people feel called, and per individual growth. Our aim is to develop each other into these specific works of ministry.


Church Distinctives

Church Leadership

We hold to the model of “elder” as opposed to “pastor” when it comes to labeling church leadership and oversight. There are a few reasons for this; one important reason is that this appears to be the term referenced for church leadership in the New Testament (Acts 20:28; Phil. 1:1; Titus 1:7). We prefer to leave the title “pastor” or “shepherd” to Jesus as our “Chief Shepherd” (1 Pet. 2:25)

In addition, we believe that biblical and effective church leadership is done through a plurality of elders, not a single CEO/Senior Pastor. This means that as we currently have only one elder leading Church of the Cross, we have temporary co-eldership coming from our sending church: Reunion Church. As we develop more elders within our church body, they will replace the co-elders from Reunion Church.

Children & Youth Ministries

Most churches offer various Sunday and mid-week ministries for youth of all ages. We hold to the view that true discipleship occurs within the complete body of Christ, not by various sub-ministries or programs. We want to encourage and cultivate an environment in which all members of the church, including our children and young adults, can participate in the Sunday corporate worship experience. As a church we have a high value for God’s Word being proclaimed, and the Lord’s Supper being partaken of together as a family.

However, we do desire for children in 5th grade and younger to experience and listen to God’s Word, and the implications of the gospel, be taught in their own language. We also want to leave open the option for all children of all ages to worship with their parents as well. We will leave this to the discretion of the parents.

Charismatic Gifts

We do affirm the continuation of all of what have been considered the “supernatural” gifts (e.g. tongues, prophecy, healing, etc.). However, we believe these are gifts that come from the Spirit (1 Cor. 12:1) for the purposes of edification for the church as the Spirit sees fit to distribute (1 Cor. 12:12-14, 14:18-19), and for the furthering of the gospel into all the world. During all formal and corporate gatherings any of these gifting’s are to be done with order and discernment (1 Cor. 14:26-33).

In order to maintain good order within the church, we will maintain a discerning posture concerning the “testing” of all things deemed spiritual without “quenching” the movement of the Spirit within our community (1 Thess. 5:19-22). Our mission will always be for the pursuit of following Christ and participating with him in his mission for San Diego.
